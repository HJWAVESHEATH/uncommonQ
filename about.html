The Story Behind the Analyst

From wrench and ledger to algorithm and model—how real-world logic shaped a career in quantitative systems.

Early Foundations

I grew up surrounded by engines, invoices, and integrity. My parents—an automotive technician and a dealership accountant—taught me that precision and honesty are two sides of the same tool. I learned to talk shop with both the crew on the floor and the finance office upstairs. That dual fluency in technical and business language still drives every decision I make.

Sales and Service

Before university, I spent a decade as a top service advisor across a multi-dealer network. The lessons were human: clarity beats jargon, and trust converts better than pressure. I discovered how data, emotion, and timing shape every decision—skills that translate directly into model communication and behavioral finance.

Finance & Accounting

Earning my degree in Accounting and completing nearly all requirements for the CPA exams refined my thinking in controls, audit trails, and risk. To me, a model is an income statement in motion—inputs, transformations, and outputs that must reconcile. The balance sheet always has to balance, whether it’s numbers or probabilities.

Quantitative Systems Architecture

Today I build frameworks that combine mathematics, data science, and business logic. My goal isn’t only predictive accuracy but explainable performance—systems that justify their behavior and earn stakeholder trust. The tools may be modern, but the values are traditional: accountability, curiosity, and craftsmanship.

Mission

To design intelligent systems that strengthen human decision-making. To use AI responsibly, transparently, and always with the goal of coherence—linking numbers, people, and outcomes into one understandable narrative.

Core Values

  • Transparency: Every calculation should be explainable and reproducible.
  • Integrity: Data work is an ethical act—honesty is the highest precision.
  • Curiosity: Questioning assumptions uncovers both error and opportunity.
  • Communication: The best models are the ones that people can understand.
